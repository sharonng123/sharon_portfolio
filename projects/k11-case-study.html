The Challenge: Bridging Digital and Physical Retail

Project Timeline: February - June 2022

As a Digital Marketing Officer at K11 MUSEA, I collaborated with the UX team to tackle a complex challenge: How do you create meaningful digital experiences that drive physical foot traffic to a premium shopping mall?

The UX Problem: Traditional marketing campaigns weren't creating lasting engagement. Users would see ads, maybe visit once, but lacked compelling reasons to return and explore different areas of the mall.

Key Challenges Identified:

  • Low repeat visit rates from digital marketing campaigns
  • Difficulty in encouraging exploration of different mall sections
  • Lack of connection between digital touchpoints and physical experiences
  • Need to appeal to tech-savvy millennials and Gen Z shoppers

The Pivot: Learning from User Testing

Our initial design was too complex. User testing revealed:

  • Cognitive Overload: Too many steps confused users
  • Technical Friction: QR code validation was unreliable
  • Delayed Gratification: Users wanted immediate rewards
UX Learning: Sometimes the best design decision is simplification. We removed complex validation steps and made rewards instant upon avatar discovery.
