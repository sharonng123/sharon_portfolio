Web3 Wallet Comparison: Onboarding & First Transactions

Role: UX Designer • Scope: Research, UX, UI • Year: 2025

Context

New users struggle with seed phrases, networks, gas fees, and transaction confirmations. Objective: reduce abandonment and errors during onboarding and the first successful transaction.

Research & Insights

  • Heuristic evaluation across 4 wallets: MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, Rabby, Phantom (EVM/SOL contrast).
  • Top issues: jargon, risky defaults, unclear network selection, and hidden fees.
  • Opportunities: progressive disclosure, secure defaults, guided recovery, and inline education.

Design Principles

  • Trust-by-design: explicit states (pending, confirmed, failed), explainers for gas/network, verification badges.
  • Progressive disclosure: start simple for novices; unlock advanced settings for experts.
  • Safety nets: warnings on risky actions, undo windows, and clear receipts.

Outcome

Redesigned flows are projected to reduce first-transaction errors and abandonment through clearer copy, defaults, and confirmations. Next steps include validation via usability testing (n=5–8).

Reflection

Future iterations will cover account recovery patterns and hardware wallet pairing guidance to increase perceived safety.
