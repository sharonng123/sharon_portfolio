The Challenge

As UX & Design Specialist at AS Watson Group, I was tasked with improving the search experience for Watsons' mobile app and website. Despite being Asia's largest health & beauty retailer, our search functionality was underperforming, leading to frustrated users and lost sales opportunities.

The Core Problem: Users were struggling to find products they wanted, leading to high search abandonment rates and decreased conversion. Our existing search relied heavily on exact keyword matches, failing to understand user intent or provide helpful suggestions.

Key Pain Points Identified:

  • Poor Search Results: 40% of searches returned irrelevant or no results
  • Complex Filtering: Users couldn't easily narrow down results by their specific needs
  • No Search Guidance: No suggestions or auto-complete to help users
  • Cultural Variations: Search behavior differed significantly across markets (Hong Kong, Thailand, Malaysia)

Phase 3: Qualitative Deep Dive

Analyzing 60+ in-depth interviews revealed four critical themes that explained the why behind user frustrations and search abandonment behaviors.

üîç Search Inefficiency

  • Keyword Mismatch: Users search in local language but products tagged in English
  • No Smart Suggestions: Typos and misspellings return zero results
  • Generic Results: Broad searches return thousands of irrelevant items

‚ö° Filter Frustration

  • Hidden Filters: Users couldn't find advanced filtering options
  • Complex Interface: Too many filter categories overwhelmed users
  • No Visual Cues: Text-heavy filters ignored by mobile users

üéØ Promotion Blindness

  • Poor Visibility: Promotions buried in search results
  • No Context: Users missed relevant deals for searched products
  • Timing Issues: Promotions not surfaced during product discovery

üåè Cultural Disconnect

  • Language Barriers: Beauty terms don't translate directly
  • Local Preferences: Search didn't understand regional skin concerns
  • Cultural Context: Western beauty categories didn't match Asian needs

Key Insight: User Archetypes

Analysis revealed two distinct user types: Browsers (explore categories casually) and Searchers (have specific intent and products in mind). Searchers represented 68% of our user base but had a 40% higher abandonment rate, indicating that our search functionality was failing our most valuable users.

What I Learned

Key UX Insights:

üß† Mental Models Matter

Users don't think in product categories - they think in problems and solutions. Design should match their mental model, not the business's inventory structure.

üìä Data + Empathy = Success

Quantitative data showed us where problems were, but qualitative research revealed why they existed and how to fix them.

üåç Cultural Sensitivity

What works in one market may fail in another. Cross-cultural research is essential for global products.

üîÑ Iterative Testing

Small improvements compound. A/B testing every change helped us optimize continuously rather than waiting for perfection.

Challenges & How I Overcame Them:

  • Technical Constraints: Worked closely with engineering to find creative solutions within existing infrastructure
  • Stakeholder Buy-in: Used data storytelling to demonstrate ROI potential and secure resources
  • Cultural Differences: Conducted region-specific research to create localized experiences
  • Legacy System Integration: Designed solutions that could work with existing backend systems while improving UX
This project taught me that great search UX isn't about advanced AI or complex algorithms - it's about understanding how people think and making technology adapt to human behavior, not the other way around.
